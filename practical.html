Practical reference

A compact field guide to the details most useful while moving through northern Italy.

Driving essentials

ZTL
Restricted historic-center traffic zone. Do not enter unless your hotel has explicitly registered your plate.
Autostrada
Take a ticket when entering and pay when exiting. Keep the ticket accessible.
Fuel
Benzina is gasoline; gasolio is diesel. Confirm the rental-car fuel type before filling.
Parking
Blue lines generally indicate paid parking; white lines may be free; yellow is usually reserved.

Emergency basics

112
Single European emergency number for police, ambulance or fire assistance.
Pharmacy
Look for the illuminated green cross; posted notices often identify the nearest after-hours pharmacy.
Documents
Keep passport copies, insurance information and rental-car documents available offline.
Hotel help
For local medical advice, transport disruptions or ZTL questions, the hotel desk is often the fastest first contact.

Fast answers on the road.

🚗

ZTL rule

Never follow navigation into a historic center unless your hotel confirms your plate has been registered.

⛽

Fuel check

Benzina means gasoline; gasolio means diesel. Photograph the fuel-door label when collecting the car.

🅿️

Parking colors

Blue is generally paid, white may be free, and yellow is normally reserved. Always read the posted sign.

🚤

Ferry routine

Screenshot the timetable, identify the return dock and keep a backup plan for wind or service changes.

💳

Paying

Carry one physical card and some euros. Choose payment in euros rather than home-currency conversion.

☕

Coffee custom

Pay first when required, keep the receipt, and order at the bar for the quickest and least expensive service.
