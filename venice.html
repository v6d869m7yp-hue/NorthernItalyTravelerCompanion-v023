v026 insider brief

Venice, made simple

Use the illustrated chapter for depth; use this panel when you need a decision quickly.

Best first moveWalk before you ride

Learn the route between your hotel, St. Mark’s and Rialto on foot; use the vaporetto later for the Grand Canal experience.

Crowd strategyEarly icons, late neighborhoods

Do St. Mark’s first, then drift toward Dorsoduro or Cannaregio after lunch.

EveningBlue hour + cicchetti

Return to the water near sunset, then eat standing at two or three small bacari rather than committing to one long meal.

Arrival rhythm

Keep the first afternoon light. Check in, orient yourself, and save the major interiors for the next morning. Venice is tiring with luggage and bridges.

Walking route

St. Mark’s → quiet lanes behind La Fenice → Accademia Bridge → Zattere → Campo Santa Margherita. Allow 2½–3 hours with stops.

Rain plan

Doge’s Palace, Scuola Grande di San Rocco, Frari, Ca’ Rezzonico or the Peggy Guggenheim. Avoid making Murano your only rainy-day solution.

Local habit

Step aside before stopping on narrow bridges, validate transport tickets, and never picnic on church steps or monument bases.

2026 planning note: overnight guests are generally exempt from Venice’s day-visitor access fee, but registration or proof requirements can still apply on designated dates. Check the official Venezia Unica rules before arrival.
