The essential Milan

Six experiences that define the city.

01

Duomo & rooftop

Go up first, while the marble forest of pinnacles is quiet. The rooftop is not an add-on; it is the most memorable part of the cathedral.

Allow 2–2½ hours
02

Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II

Look up, not only into the shop windows. Visit once in daylight and again after dinner when the mosaic floor and iron-and-glass vault glow.

20–40 minutes
03

Leonardo’s Last Supper

A tightly timed visit requiring advance reservations. Pair it with nearby Santa Maria delle Grazie and San Maurizio.

Book well ahead
04

La Scala

The museum provides access to the historic auditorium when rehearsals allow. An evening performance transforms the experience.

45–75 minutes
05

Brera

Cobbled lanes, galleries, restrained elegance and one of the city’s best late-afternoon walks. Let this be a neighborhood, not merely a museum stop.

2–3 hours
06

Navigli at aperitivo

Walk the quieter side streets before settling beside the canal. Choose atmosphere over the biggest buffet.

Arrive before sunset

Eat and drink

Order like you came for Milan.

Risotto alla Milanese

Saffron, marrow-rich depth and proper all’onda texture.

Cotoletta

Bone-in, butter-fried and ideally crisp at the edges.

Ossobuco

Often paired with saffron risotto; a cool-weather classic.

Aperitivo

One good drink with intentional small plates—not a race through a buffet.

Getting around

Walk the center; use transit to connect districts.

The historic core is compact. Save the Metro and trams for the jumps between the Duomo, Porta Venezia, Porta Nuova and the Navigli.

Walk

Duomo, Galleria, La Scala and Brera combine naturally on foot.

Metro

Fastest for crossing the city and reaching the main rail stations.

Historic tram

Useful when its route aligns—and an experience in its own right.

Taxi

Most valuable for luggage, late evenings and tight reservation windows.
